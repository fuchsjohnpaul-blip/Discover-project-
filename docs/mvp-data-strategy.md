# MVP Data Strategy

## Direction

The first version will launch with a small, hand-picked list of trusted restaurants in Tuscaloosa, Alabama.

This is intentionally a curation-first product strategy, not a marketplace strategy.

## Why This Is The Right MVP

- It keeps quality higher than broad directory-style apps
- It makes manual review realistic
- It reduces the chance of showing misleading gluten-free results
- It gives us a cleaner foundation for future expansion

## What "Trusted" Means In The MVP

A restaurant should only be included in the first release if we have enough evidence to feel comfortable presenting it to users.

That evidence may include:

- Current menu text
- Restaurant gluten-free labeling
- Direct restaurant website information
- Internal review notes
- Human approval in our admin process

## What We Curate

For each restaurant, we should aim to capture:

- Basic restaurant identity and location details
- Restaurant-level gluten-free category
- Individual menu items
- Confidence level for each item
- Source links or source notes
- Caution notes for cross-contact or ambiguity
- Review status and last-reviewed date

## Product Tradeoff

Users may see fewer restaurants at first, but the results should feel much more dependable.

That tradeoff supports the brand promise of trust and safety.

## Operational Model For The First Launch

- Seed the database with a starter Tuscaloosa list
- Review menu items manually before publishing them
- Use a clear publish flag so only approved data is shown in the app
- Track when each restaurant and menu item was last reviewed

## Future Expansion Path

After the curated Tuscaloosa launch proves the workflow, we can expand in stages:

1. Add more Tuscaloosa restaurants
2. Add nearby cities or regions
3. Support more dietary restrictions
4. Introduce more automation in source ingestion and review
